Studies in Modernism

This course will introduce modernism as a set of aesthetic and cultural movements that responded to tremendous social changes and political tumults in the first half of the twentieth century. We will survey some major contributions to modernist fiction and poetry by British, American, and Canadian artists. We will focus on developments in narrative and technique, critical statements by modernist artists, and the cultural contexts that gave rise to them. We will also situate modernism in relation to nineteenth-century realism and postmodernism. The focus will be on several key figures, striving for a balance between male and female, and straight and queer, artists. Questions of empire, colonialism, emergent global capitalism, sex and sexuality, subjectivity, gender, war and violence, race and racism, nationhood, and cosmopolitanism will feature prominently.
