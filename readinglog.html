Reading Log

— brief records of the books I've read recently (no spoilers) —

2020

fiction

  • Joanna Russ, The Female Man (1975) and Extra(ordinary) People (1985) – Both are wonderfully mind-boggling books for good reasons
  • Jeanette Winterson, The Passion (1987)
  • Lewis Grassic Gibbon, A Scots Quair (a trilogy: 1932–1934) and Spartacus (1933)
  • Vita Sackville-West, All Passion Spent (1931) – an extremely interesting storyline!
  • Virginia Woolf, Three Guineas (1938) and The Waves (1931) – one of my favorite writers; these two later works represent a more explicitly political and experimental Woolf
  • Zadie Smith, White Teeth (2000) – this funny, moving, satirical, beautiful book has left me with many emotions
  • Ursula K. Le Guin, The Left Hand of Darkness (1969) – always worth re-reading – and of course The Unreal and the Real (vol.2)
  • Ford Madox Ford, The Marsden Case (1923) and Parade's End (a tetralogy: 1924-1928; BBC has a mini series of the tetralogy – Benedict Cumberbatch as Christopher Tietjens)
  • Margaret Atwood, The Testaments (2019)
  • Tommy Orange, There There (2008) – everyone should read it
  • Peg Tittle, It Wasn't Enough (available as an e-book on kindle now)
  • Ali Smith, Autumn (2016) – extremely moving, lyrical, and funny; I listened to the audio book version first and the got a physical copy of the book to read it again.
  • Kim Stanley Robinson, New York 2140 (2017) – without spoiling the story, I would just say that it's a brilliant thought experiment

poetry

  • W. H. Auden, The Age of Anxiety: A Baroque Eclogue (1947)
  • H.D., Trilogy (1946) and Helen in Egypt (New Directions 1961)

others
